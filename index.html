Learning Objective

A fun Fractions group activity is presented in a fun back to school activity format for grade 5 students. This is a great way to enrich 4th grade, teach 5th grade, and provide intervention for 6th grade!

Product Description

Fractions group activity for 5th grade students just got a lot more fun! Perfect for a back to school activity (or anytime, really!), these math task cards are actually an entertaining game of school days mystery!

These back to school math activities will get your fifth grade students engaged and learning in those first challenging weeks of school - with very little prep from you! We all love doing back to school activities that help jump start our math understanding while playing a fun game at the same time. A perfect bridge from a summer at home to back to school!

Select from color or black and white task cards for this back to school activity. Students can play with partners, small groups, or the entire class.

These puzzles help your students practice their fraction skills, including multiplying and dividing fractions, and adding and subtracting fractions - plus they gain new confidence in their growing math skills. These are 5th grade math task cards in a fun and flexible game format!

This is a great way to enrich 4th grade, teach 5th grade, and provide intervention for 6th grade!

Here's how it works: Each of the four puzzles in this collection is aligned to a different Common Core standard and contains 12 fraction problems to solve. The correct answer for each math problem will allow the student to eliminate one or more "suspects" from the school-themed mysteries. Only when every math problem is answered correctly will the student be able to solve the mystery. It grades itself!

What you get: There is a total of 48 math problems. You get both color and black and white task cards for easy printing - 4 task cards per sheet, 12 task cards per puzzle. And because these math task cards adhere to Common Core standards, we print them on the footer of all pages so you can find them in a hurry. You also get recording sheets and answer keys.

Here's how you can use these new math task cards:

  • You can laminate them for use over and over.
  • Print the black and white version on colored paper for ink-savings.
  • Whip out these school-themed task cards as a fun bell-ringer, a non-food reward, or as a math center.
  • Project the cards for whole group learning - the mystery theme makes it a fun group activity as well.

Each of the 4 puzzles takes about 20 minutes to complete, so you have a lot of flexibility built in to the set.

Common Core Alignment:

CCSS.Math.Content.5.NF.A.1
Add and subtract fractions with unlike denominators (including mixed numbers) by replacing given fractions with equivalent fractions in such a way as to produce an equivalent sum or difference of fractions with like denominators.

CCSS.Math.Content.5.NF.B.4
Apply and extend previous understandings of multiplication to multiply a fraction or whole number by a fraction.

CCSS.Math.Content.5.NF.B.7.a
Interpret division of a unit fraction by a non-zero whole number, and compute such quotients.

CCSS.Math.Content.5.NF.B.7.b
Interpret division of a whole number by a unit fraction, and compute such quotients.
